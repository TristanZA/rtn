Used Truck Sales Remain Buoyant 

Tristan Wiggill April 24, 2021 Truck Auctions

Park Village Auctions has secured yet another collection of quality used commercial vehicles, which is sure to satisfy a continued demand among dealers, fleet and logistics managers across the board.

So says PVA’s Natashia Goosen.

“While sales statistics for new commercial vehicles have only recently started recovering, used vehicle sales have remained buoyant. I believe that this will continue for some time as buyer confidence and cash flow are still under pressure. 

“Discerning fleet managers are turning to auctions to replenish their fleet with newer models in light of the global stock shortage on certain vehicles.”

Related: Actros Fleet The Highlight At Truck Auction

As ever, the auction will bring to market assorted trucks, trailers, earthmoving and agricultural equipment stemming from genuine bank repossessions, business liquidation and deceased estate matters.

Speaking about highlights for the upcoming auction, Goosen singles out a fleet of 2018 Mercedes-Benz Actros 2646's and 3344's; a 2016/17 Mercedes-Benz Marcopolo 52-seater bus, and a 2018 Revaro hollow brick making machine.

After the Actros fleet, remaining truck tractor stock includes a 2017 UD Quon GW 26-450, a 2018 Volvo FMX 440, a 2016 Hino 500 1726, a Scania G460, a MAN TGS 17-374, an Iveco Stralis 480, various Freightliner Cat and Argosy's and an International Pro Sleeper.

Rigid trucks to be sold include a 2019 Isuzu FTS 750 and a 2017 Isuzu NPR 400, an assortment of MAN TGM 25-280, a Powerstar 4035, a FAW 8-140, a Mercedes-Benz 1617, a Hino 300, a Nissan UD40, a Mitsubishi and a Toyota Dyna. 

A 2018 Henred Fruehauf flatdeck, a 2015 Afrit dropside, a 2015 Top Trailers side tipper are available, as are Paramount, Kearny, Roadhog and SA Tuck Bodies trailers.

Other noteworthy lots include a Case JX90 Tractor, a 2017 Iveco 70C15 panel van, a Caterpillar 226B skidsteer and various brands of forklifts.
